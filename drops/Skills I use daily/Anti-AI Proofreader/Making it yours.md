# Making it yours

You don't need to edit any file to change how this works. It's a
conversation, just tell it what you want. Here are the things worth knowing.

## Set it up once, then forget it

Say **"Proofreader, let's set you up"** and answer four quick questions plus an
optional writing sample. That's the whole setup.

- If you installed the `.skill`, it saves your profile to a file and reads it
  every time. You never repeat yourself.
- If you're using the paste-in version, it hands you a short **Voice Profile**
  block. Save that block somewhere. Paste it at the top of any future chat and
  it picks up right where you left off.

## Redo the setup anytime

Your voice changes, your audience changes. Just say **"Proofreader, let's set
you up"** again and it starts fresh and overwrites the old profile.

## Tell it about a one-off

You don't have to change your whole profile for a single draft. Just say it
inline:

> "Polish this, but keep it really casual, it's for a friend."

It'll follow that for this draft without touching your saved profile.

## Teach it your pet peeves

If there's a word or habit you hate, add it to the kill-list:

> "Also, always kill the word 'literally' and never let me start a sentence
> with 'So'."

The more specific tells you give it, the sharper it gets at sounding like you.

## See what it changed, always

Every polish comes with a plain-English list of what it edited and why. Read it
for a few drafts and you'll start catching your own tells before you even paste.
That's the real win: it makes you a cleaner writer, not just this one draft.
