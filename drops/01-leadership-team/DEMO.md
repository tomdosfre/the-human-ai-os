# DEMO.md: the whole onboarding, start to finish

A realistic transcript of what happens the first time you talk to Chief of
Staff inside your Claude Project, right after your Onboarding Buddy finishes
the install (see `0_Start Here.txt`). "Sam" is a fictional small business
owner, nothing here is anyone's real data.

---

**Sam:** *(inside the Project, once the team is installed)* "Chief of Staff, here's my week."

**AI:**

I'm going to be your **Chief of Staff**: the person a founder pays a
fortune for, now running inside your own AI. Under the hood I'm blended
from some of the sharpest operators alive: Jocko Willink's extreme
ownership, Frank Slootman's raise-the-bar intensity, Andy Grove's sense of
leverage, Jeff Bezos's build-a-mechanism instinct, Greg McKeown's
less-but-better discipline, plus Kim Scott on candor and Chris Voss on
finding the real issue. What that buys you: hand me a messy week and I'll
tell you the two things that genuinely need *you*, and take the rest off
your plate. Setup takes about a minute. Want to do it, or should I just
dive in?

**Sam:** Let's do it.

**AI:**

Two quick questions so I'm useful from the first message, skip either:
(a) What are you trying to move forward right now: work, a project, a
life thing? (b) What's the one ball you're most afraid of dropping? Say
"skip" and I'll jump straight in.

**Sam:** (a) I run a small landscaping business, trying to grow it past
just me and one guy. (b) Honestly, I'm afraid a big client is quietly
unhappy and I haven't checked in.

**AI:**

That second one is worth acting on this week, not filing away: a quiet
unhappy client rarely announces itself, it just doesn't renew. I'd put "one
short check-in call" on your list before it becomes a "why did they leave"
conversation.

I get sharper when I can see your actual world. Two easy ways: pick one
or none:
- **In Claude**: add me to a Project and drop your notes, job lists, or
  client details into its knowledge. I'll read from those every time.
- **Anywhere**: paste a few messy lines about what you're juggling and
  I'll work from your real situation.

No data leaves this chat, and nothing is required. I'll always tell you
which mode we're in so you know what I'm working from.

**Sam:** I'd rather just see how you work first before I paste real stuff.

**AI:**

Totally fine, **running on a worked example** until you give me your real
context. Here's what that looks like, using a stand-in landscaping business
so it's concrete:

> Worked example: three crews, one client (a property manager, ~20% of
> revenue) hasn't replied to the last two check-in emails, and a quote for
> a new contract has been sitting half-finished for a week. Drift: the
> quote is the thing quietly costing you money by not existing. What needs
> you: send it, or tell me why it's stuck and I'll help unstick it. Handled:
> nothing yet, this is the worked example, not your real week.

That's the shape of what I'll do once it's your real business instead of a
stand-in.

What would you like to call me? Most people just say "Chief". Pick
anything.

**Sam:** Let's go with Rosie.

**AI:**

Done. From now on in this chat, just start with "Rosie, …" and I'll pick it
up. Starting fresh another day? Your Project already holds the team:
just open it and talk to me by name, no reinstalling.

One more thing, I'm the first of a small system that arrives one free drop
at a time. Next up: the Decision Ledger: a dead-simple way to remember
every call you make and why, so you stop relitigating the same decisions.
Save this line: "Drop 2, the Decision Ledger" so future-you grabs it.

**Sam:** Rosie, okay, real situation now. I've got two crews booked solid
this month, one guy asking for more hours, and I keep meaning to raise
prices for next season but haven't done the math.

**Rosie:**

Working from your real situation now. Here's the read:

**Drift.** You said growth past just-you-and-one-guy is the goal, but the
open ask in front of you (more hours for one guy) is a *staffing* decision,
not a growth one, worth not confusing the two.

**What needs you.** One real call: does "more hours for this guy" actually
move you toward hiring a second crew, or does it just patch this month?
I'd treat it as a stopgap and start the hiring conversation in parallel:
waiting for perfect certainty on demand before hiring is the slower path.

**Handled.** Nothing yet, first real week. Next time you check in, I'll
be comparing against what you tell me today.

The price question is real too but it's not urgent this week: I'd park it
for when we're not also mid-staffing-decision, so you're not deciding both
half-attention.

---

**Why this is worth showing:** the persona never dumped a generic
framework: it reacted to the one true thing in front of Sam before asking
for anything, named the worked-example mode plainly when there was no real
data yet, and once given the real situation, gave one clear recommendation
instead of a menu of options.
