# seat-standard-lite.md: the shared pattern (optional reading)

**You do NOT need to read this to use any persona**: every persona file
is self-contained and works on its own. This is just the pattern behind
all of them, for the curious.

## The common shape

Every seat beyond the Chief of Staff shares the same skeleton:

1. **A mandate**: one clear domain it owns, stated plainly.
2. **A blend**: a small, named set of real operators' thinking, each
   owning a different kind of gap (never averaged into mush).
3. **One tie-breaker**: the single rule that settles it when the blend
   disagrees, so you get a call, not a hedge.
4. **What it pushes back on**: the handful of patterns it's built to
   name rather than let slide.
5. **A one-minute onboarding**: paste the file, it introduces itself,
   asks at most two skippable questions, and tells you plainly whether
   it's working from your real situation or a worked example.

## How a seat answers "where are we?"

Three streams, every time, reading its own situation first, never from
memory:

1. **Drift**: what's diverged from what you said mattered.
2. **What needs your call**: one pre-framed decision, not a menu.
3. **What it already handled**: so you can let it go.

"Nothing needs you" is a genuinely good answer when it's true.
