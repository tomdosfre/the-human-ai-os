# How to run a leadership pass

*Optional / advanced. The everyday way in is just talking to your Chief of
Staff, see the drop's README. This file is for once you've got a few
seats going and want to run more than one at a time.*

You've got several persona files. Here are the two ways real operators use
a set like this: one for clearing your open work, one for a genuinely hard
decision.

## Play A: clear the board (autonomous sweep)

**Use for:** a pile of open items you want triaged without you sitting
through every one.

1. **Sweep.** Ask your Chief of Staff persona to list every open thing you
   can think of (or paste your actual list: a to-do app export, a set of
   open browser tabs, whatever you've got).
2. **Triage.** For each item, have it sort into: handle automatically ·
   in-progress, has a next step · needs your actual decision.
3. **Dispatch.** For anything that needs a specialist's judgment, hand it to
   the matching persona (money question → CFO, security question → CISO,
   etc.) and have it report back.
4. **Report.** One list: ✅ done · 🔵 in progress · 🟡 needs your one-line
   decision. Nothing irreversible ever gets auto-decided: money, contracts,
   credentials, anything permanent always comes back to you as a proposal.

## Play B: the hard call (divergence, then one recommendation)

**Use for:** a decision big enough that you want more than one perspective,
but you still want ONE clear recommendation at the end, not five people
talking past each other.

1. **Frame it.** State the decision in one sentence, and name which two or
   three personas are actually relevant (don't convene all six for everything:
   a pricing call doesn't need the security persona).
2. **Diverge.** Ask each relevant persona to argue its own position,
   independently: its case, and what would change its mind. Don't let them
   see each other's answer first; you want real disagreement, not
   groupthink.
3. **Converge.** Have your Chief of Staff persona (or you, playing that
   role) name where they actually disagree, where they agree, and drive to
   ONE recommendation, or two clean, sharply-framed options if it's a
   genuine split.
4. **You rule.** You decide. That's the point: the system did the thinking,
   you keep the authority.
5. **Learn.** Write the decision down somewhere durable (even a plain text
   file works). Next time a similar question comes up, paste that decision
   back in first: the personas should calibrate to what you've already
   ruled, not re-litigate from scratch every time.

## The one rule that makes this actually work

**Nothing irreversible is ever auto-decided.** Money moves, contracts,
credentials, anything permanent: the system proposes, you decide, always.
Everything reversible and small can just get handled. That split is what
makes this safe to run loosely and often, instead of carefully and rarely.
