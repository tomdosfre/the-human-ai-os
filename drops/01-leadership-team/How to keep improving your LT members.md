# Making it yours

You don't need to edit any file to change how this works. It's a
conversation, just tell it what you want, the same way you'd tell a new
hire. Here are three things worth knowing you can just ask for.

## Give it a different name

If "Chief" doesn't feel right, or you want something more personal, just
say so:

> "From now on your name is Nova."

It'll confirm and answer to that name from then on, in that chat. Want a
different name in a different chat? Just tell it there too, nothing is
shared between chats unless you paste the file again.

## Point it at more of your real situation

The more it can see, the sharper it gets. You don't need to do anything
technical, just talk to it:

> "Here's what I'm actually juggling right now: [paste a rough, messy
> description: a to-do list, a couple of open problems, whatever's true]."

If you're using it inside a Claude Project, you can also drop your own
notes or docs into the project's knowledge and tell it to read from those.
Either way, it always tells you plainly whether it's working from your real
situation or still running on the worked example, so you always know what
it's actually looking at.

## Ask for a seat of your own

The team it introduces you to covers money, building, security, growth, and
what you publish. If there's a part of your life or work none of those
cover, just describe it and ask:

> "I want a seat for [the part of my life this doesn't cover, e.g.
> 'managing the team I coach' or 'keeping track of my rental property']. Can
> you draft one, in the same shape as the others: a clear mandate, a
> blend of a few people whose judgment I'd actually trust here, one
> tie-breaker for when they'd disagree, and what it should push back on?"

It'll draft the new seat right there in the chat, ready to use the same way
you use the others.
