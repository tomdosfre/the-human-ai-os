# Demo: the Anti-AI Proofreader in action

A short, real example of what you get. Your setup takes about a minute; after
that, every polish looks like this.

## Setup (once)

> **You:** Proofreader, let's set you up.
>
> **Proofreader:** Nice, this takes about a minute. Four quick things:
> 1. Tone: how do you want to come across? (polished / warm / punchy / academic)
> 2. Audience: who are you usually writing for?
> 3. Style: how much emoji and formatting feels like you?
> 4. Always strip: which should I always remove: buzzwords, "not X but Y",
>    em-dashes, corporate jargon, hype?
>
> **You:** Warm. LinkedIn. Light touch. Strip buzzwords, "not X but Y", and hype.
>
> **Proofreader:** Got it. Paste 1 or 2 things you've written that sound like
> you, or say "skip".
>
> **You:** skip
>
> **Proofreader:** You're set. Here's your Voice Profile, save it and paste it
> into future chats: [tone: warm · audience: LinkedIn · style: light touch ·
> kill-list: buzzwords, negative parallelism, hype]. Paste any draft and say
> "Proofreader" whenever you're ready.

## Polishing a draft

> **You:** Proofreader, clean this up:
>
> *"In today's fast-paced world, I'm super excited to share that we've decided
> to leverage a cutting-edge new framework to unlock next-level productivity.
> It's not just about the tools, it's about the mindset. This is a total
> game-changer!!"*

**What it hands back:**

> We rebuilt how the team plans its week, and the first month already freed up
> about a day each. The tool matters less than the habit it forced: deciding
> what actually needs a human before touching the software.

**What changed:**

- **Throat-clearing:** cut "In today's fast-paced world" and "I'm super excited
  to share."
- **Buzzwords:** removed "leverage", "cutting-edge", "unlock", "next-level."
- **Negative parallelism:** rewrote "it's not just about the tools, it's about
  the mindset" into a direct statement.
- **Hype:** dropped "total game-changer!!" and the exclamation marks.
- **Vague claim, made concrete:** "next-level productivity" became a real result
  ("freed up about a day each"). *(Swap in your own true number here, I flagged
  it because the original didn't say what actually happened.)*

The rewrite is shorter, says something specific, and still sounds like a person.
That's the whole idea.
